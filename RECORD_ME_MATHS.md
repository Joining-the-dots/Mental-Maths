# 🎙️ RECORD ME — every maths trick, the full lesson version

Each passage is now a mini-lesson: a challenge to hook them in, the trick,
one worked example, two for the child to try, then both checked together —
with encouragement all the way through. Read it like you’re at the kitchen
table and someone just asked "go on then, show me".

## The four rules

1. Before each passage say its number clearly — **"Number one"**, **"Number two"** —
   pause a second, read the passage, then pause a couple of seconds before the
   next number. Those gaps are how I find the joins.
2. Wherever you see **…** leave a short beat — two seconds is plenty. Those are
   the "have a think" moments for the kids.
3. If you stumble, don’t stop the recording: pause, say the SAME number again,
   and read that passage again. I’ll keep your retake.
4. Kitchen-table warmth beats newsreader polish every time.

**Total is roughly two hours of reading — absolutely fine to do over several
sittings** (each take just needs to start at a numbered item; tick them off on
the printed version as you go). When done, drop the audio file(s) — m4a, mp3,
wav, anything — into OneDrive `Times tables/recordings` and tell me.
