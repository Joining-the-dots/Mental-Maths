    🔢 Major System
    Turn numbers into words by swapping each digit for a consonant sound.

    Each digit has its own consonant sound. To remember a number, mash up the consonants into a word — vowels (a, e, i, o, u) and silent letters don't count, so you can sprinkle them in for free.

    0
    S · Z
    "zero" starts with Z
    1
    T · D
    one downstroke, like a "t"
    2
    N
    "n" has 2 downstrokes
    3
    M
    "m" has 3 downstrokes
    4
    R
    "four" ends in R
    5
    L
    Roman numeral L = 50
    6
    SH · CH · J
    "J" looks a bit like a 6
    7
    K · C · G
    two 7s back-to-back make a K
    8
    F · V
    cursive "f" looks like an 8
    9
    P · B
    "P" mirrored looks like 9

    Free letters (vowels & helpers)

    Sprinkle these in anywhere — they don't change the number:

    A · E · I · O · U · W · H · Y

    How it works
